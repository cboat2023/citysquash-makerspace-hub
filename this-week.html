Current workshop

LED and Push-Button Challenge

Learn how a button sends input to a circuit, then use it to control an LED in simulation and on a breadboard.

01

This week’s goal

Build a reliable input-and-output circuit and explain what changes when the button is pressed.

02

Required reading

Review Makerspace Safety and the LED, resistor, push button, and breadboard entries in the Component Guide.

03

Required Tinkercad tutorials

  1. Complete the introductory circuits lesson.
  2. Build an LED circuit with a current-limiting resistor.
  3. Add a push button and test both button states.
Open Tinkercad Classroom
04

Physical build challenge

Rebuild your working simulation with real components. Disconnect power before moving wires, and ask an instructor to check your circuit before powering it.

05

Completion checklist

  • The simulation runs without errors
  • The button changes the LED state
  • The physical circuit matches the plan
  • The circuit works three times in a row
  • The station is clean and parts are returned
06

Evidence to show

  • Your saved Tinkercad design
  • A working physical circuit
  • A short explanation of the button, resistor, and LED
  • One problem you found and how you tested it
Finished?

Try an extension

Reverse the button behavior, add a second LED, or turn the circuit into a reaction-timer idea. Get instructor approval before adding unfamiliar parts.
