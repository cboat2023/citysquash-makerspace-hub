Week 2 — Tuesday

Review the foundations, finish Tinkercad assignments, submit progress evidence, and prepare for Chapter 2.

Today’s agenda60 minutes
Today’s goal

Strengthen the Week 1 foundations

Before learning new material, we are making sure everyone understands the Week 1 electricity concepts and has completed the required Tinkercad work.

By the end of class, students should:

  • Review Manga Guide to Electricity Chapter 1.
  • Participate in the Week 1 electricity review game.
  • Complete or make progress on their Tinkercad assignments.
  • Submit evidence of their work.
  • Know their first task for Thursday.
Today’s agenda

Follow these steps in order

Each block includes its time. Keep moving so there is time to submit your work.

  1. 1
    Review

    Manga Chapter 1 Review

    5 minutes

    Review these ideas together:

    • What is electricity?
    • What are electrons?
    • What is electric charge?
    • What is the difference between positive and negative charge?
    • What is a conductor?
    • What is an insulator?

    Instructor note: Students should answer first before the instructor explains or corrects the answer.

  2. 2
    Move and think

    Week 1 Game: Electricity Corners

    10 minutes

    Label four areas of the room: Positive, Negative, Conductor, and Insulator. Move to the corner that matches your answer.

    Charge Questions

    A proton has what type of charge?

    Show answer

    Positive

    An electron has what type of charge?

    Show answer

    Negative

    What happens when two electrons come close together?

    Show answer

    They repel.

    What happens when a positive and negative charge come close together?

    Show answer

    They attract.

    Conductor or Insulator

    Copper wire

    Show answer

    Conductor

    Metal paper clip

    Show answer

    Conductor

    Plastic ruler

    Show answer

    Insulator

    Rubber

    Show answer

    Insulator

    Aluminum foil

    Show answer

    Conductor

    Wooden craft stick

    Show answer

    Usually an insulator

    Coin

    Show answer

    Conductor

    Glass

    Show answer

    Insulator

    Final Challenge

    Why are electrical wires made from metal but covered with plastic or rubber?

    Show expected answer

    The metal conducts electricity, while the plastic or rubber helps prevent people and other objects from touching the electrical current.

  3. 3
    Main work block

    Tinkercad Catch-Up Block

    35 minutes

    Work through this priority order:

    1. Finish unfinished Week 1 Tinkercad tutorials.
    2. Repair any circuit that did not work.
    3. Complete the battery, LED, and resistor circuit.
    4. Complete the breadboard activity.
    5. Add a switch or pushbutton.
    6. Rename and save each completed design.
    Open Tinkercad Circuits
    Finished early?

    Switch Challenge

    Build a circuit with a power source, LED, resistor, and switch. The switch must control whether the LED turns on or off.

    Then answer:

    • Where does electricity begin?
    • What path does it follow?
    • What happens when the switch is open?
    • What happens when the switch is closed?
    • Why is the resistor included?

    Try your team first: Ask another student for help before asking the instructor, unless the problem involves safety or damaged equipment.

  4. 4
    Document your work

    Progress Evidence

    5 minutes

    Submit:

    • A screenshot of your best working circuit.
    • The name of the tutorial or activity.
    • What you completed today.
    • One problem you encountered.
    • What you will work on next.
    “Today I completed __________. My circuit works because __________. Next class I need to __________.”
    Open Progress Tracker
  5. 5
    Reflect and plan

    Exit Ticket

    5 minutes
    1. What is the difference between a conductor and an insulator?
    2. Why is metal used inside electrical wires?
    3. Name one thing you completed in Tinkercad today.
    4. What is your first task for Thursday?
    Complete Student Check-In
